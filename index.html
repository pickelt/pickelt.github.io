           Valorant


Overview

Valorant is a 5v5 character-based tactical shooter where teams of Attackers and Defenders complete objectives, primarily planting or defusing a Spike (bomb) on a designated site. Players earn in-game currency to buy weapons, shields, and abilities each round, and they choose from a diverse roster of Agents, each possessing unique abilities that add a strategic layer to the core gunplay. The game requires precise aiming, strategic movement, and coordinated team abilities to win rounds and ultimately secure a match victory.

Origins

Valorant was developed and published by Riot Games, the same company behind the popular game League of Legends. The game was officially announced in October 2019 and released on June 2, 2020. Valorant was created to fill a niche in the competitive gaming market, combining elements of tactical shooters like Counter-Strike with hero-based gameplay similar to Overwatch. Riot Games aimed to create a game that emphasized skill, strategy, and teamwork while also being accessible to a wide audience. The game's development focused on creating a balanced and competitive environment, with regular updates and new content to keep the player base engaged.
